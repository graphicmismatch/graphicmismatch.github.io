Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 30th April 2025

At GraphicMismatch, your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy outlines what data we collect through our game and how it is used.

1. Information We Collect

We do not collect any personal data directly from players. However, our game integrates third-party services provided by Unity Technologies, which may collect certain data automatically for analytics and advertising purposes.

These services include:

  • Unity Analytics
  • Unity Ads

2. Third-Party Services

Unity Analytics

Unity Analytics collects anonymized gameplay and device data, such as:

  • Device type and operating system version
  • Gameplay events (e.g., level completion, session duration)
  • IP address (used to determine approximate location)

More information can be found in Unity’s privacy policy:
https://unity.com/legal/privacy-policy

Unity Ads

Unity Ads may collect information to display relevant in-game ads, such as, but not limited to:

  • Advertising ID (IDFA/AAID)
  • Unity Player ID
  • Device specifications
  • A unique Unity Authentication Service ID (UAS ID) is generated per user
  • General location (derived from IP address)

You can manage ad tracking preferences in your device settings:

  • iOS:
    • Settings > Privacy > Advertising
  • Android:
    • Settings > Google > Ads
    • Settings > Security/Privacy > Ads

3. Children’s Privacy

We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under the age of 13 (or the applicable age in your jurisdiction). If we become aware that such information has been collected, we will take immediate steps to delete it.

4. Your Rights and Choices

Since we do not collect personal data directly, your rights regarding data management lie with Unity. Please review Unity’s privacy policy for more information on opting out of data collection.

5. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted within the game or on this page. Continued use of the game implies acceptance of the updated policy.
